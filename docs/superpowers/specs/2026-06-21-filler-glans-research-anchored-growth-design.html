Filler Simulator — Research-Anchored Glans (Head) Growth

Date: 2026-06-21 · Status: Approved by user (Q&A locked below) · Implementation plan: docs/superpowers/plans/2026-06-21-filler-glans-research-anchored-growth.html (next) · Scope: filler files only (frontend, standalone bundle) — no Firestore, no rules, no deploy this turn.

Purpose / Problem

The current glans model treats the head as saturating at ~2 mL (a hidden "medical ΔØ" plateau + a separate saturating "visual" exponential). The owner's clinic routinely injects up to 10 cc into the glans and it visibly grows — so a plateau is wrong. Filler is not a black hole: "the gel physically occupies space within the tissue… the filler itself is the volume increase." The "2 mL plateau" was a misreading of the literature: 2 mL is simply the only glans dose ever studied (no published glans study exceeds 3 mL), not a point where growth stops.

Fix: replace the dual saturating model with one honest, research-anchored, volume-conservation (cube-root) model that keeps growing with injected cc, anchored to the measured 2 cc data point, capped at a 15 cc head-injection lock (to bound the exaggeration). The 2D/3D head + a numeric durable→peak readout must reflect this real ratio.

The model

Head is a compact body, so injected volume → diameter grows as the cube root of total volume (never plateaus; each cc adds a little less Ø — real tissue physics, same volume-geometry family as the existing shaft model). Two curves (durable / peak) anchored to the two 2 cc data points.

glansDiameterGain(cc, dg0, veff) = dg0 · ( cbrt(1 + max(cc,0)/veff) − 1 )

veff calibrated so 2 cc lands on the study Ø change at the default glans baseline (dg0 ≈ 3.5 cm):
  peak    : 2 cc → +0.53 cm Ø (+15.1%)  → veff_peak    ≈ 3.81
  durable : 2 cc → +0.45 cm Ø (+12.8%)  → veff_durable ≈ 4.60
(exact veff recomputed + locked by the 2-cc anchor test in the plan; integer-fraction literals so the build obfuscator hides them.)

Scales with the user's chosen baseline (glansBaseRatio × shaft Ø) because dg0 multiplies the gain.
DELETE: GLANS_SATURATION_CC plateau · GLANS_VISUAL_MAX_DELTA · GLANS_VISUAL_HALF_CC · glansVisualGain · the dgLow/dgHigh "medical" split.
KEEP field names visualLow/visualHigh (now = durable / peak Ø) so the 2D/3D consumers need no change (minimal diff).
Glans stays independent of girth / condom (unchanged).

Acceptance Criteria

  1. estimate({glansCc:2}) → glans durable ΔØ ≈ +0.45 cm and peak ≈ +0.53 cm (±0.03) on the default baseline.
  2. Glans Ø is strictly increasing from 0→15 cc (no plateau): ΔØ(10) > ΔØ(5) > ΔØ(2), and ΔØ(15) > ΔØ(10).
  3. Per-cc gain diminishes (cube-root): ΔØ(4)−ΔØ(2) < ΔØ(2)−ΔØ(0).
  4. peak > durable at every cc > 0.
  5. Split slider cannot set glans > 15 cc even when total > 15.
  6. Changing glansCc does NOT change girth / condom width outputs.
  7. 2D mushroom + 3D bulb visibly enlarge at 2 / 10 / 15 cc (rendered-pixel verified).
  8. (user #3) 2D exact scale: shaft Ø, glans Ø, and length are all rendered at ONE px-per-cm; the measured glans-Ø-px ÷ shaft-Ø-px equals the math glans.visualLow ÷ d1Low ratio (±2%), and length-px ÷ shaft-Ø-px equals lengthCm ÷ d1Low (±2%). Rendered-pixel verified.
  9. (user #3) 3D exact scale: in the built model, glansR ÷ shaft-r equals visualLow ÷ (c1Low/π) (math ratio). Rendered-pixel verified at 2/10/15cc.
  10. (user #2) No "ระยะคงตัว → ช่วงแรก (1 เดือน)" caption appears under the head readout — only the numeric range.
  11. (user #1) Website refs modal AND the PDF both show the new glans model + the anchor source + the ≤3mL/extrapolation caveat; the PDF's glans worked-example numbers equal estimate() output for that example cc.

Risks / Open Questions

  • Extrapolation honesty — biggest risk is over-claiming at 10–15 cc where no glans study exists. Mitigated by the explicit caveat + cube-root (conservative, diminishing) + shaft cross-validation. Do NOT present 10–15 cc numbers as "proven".
  • Default baseline assumption — veff is calibrated at dg0 ≈ 3.5 cm so the 2 cc anchor reproduces the study's absolute Ø; for other baselines it scales by ratio (defensible). The 2-cc anchor test pins the exact veff.
  • Obfuscator — keep constants as integer fractions (e.g. 381/100) so numbersToExpressions hides them, per the existing fillerMath convention.
  • Visual shape choice — 2D/3D render the durable Ø (honest settled look); the readout shows the full durable→peak range. (If owner prefers the visual to show peak, it's a one-line swap.)
  • 2D exact-scale = uniform per-render fit — a single s (px/cm) chosen so the current model fills the band keeps internal proportions geometrically true (glans-vs-shaft, length-vs-girth), then auto-fits absolute size — exactly mirroring how the 3D auto-frames. A fixed absolute px/cm can't fit the full input range (2.5–10 in length × full girth × +15cc head) in a fixed viewBox, so per-render uniform scale is the correct analog. Cross-section keeps the same s for consistency.
